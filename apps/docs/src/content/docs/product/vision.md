# Scrollect — Product Vision Draft

## One-Liner

Turn your scrolling habit into a learning habit — a personalized, AI-powered feed built from the content you actually care about.

## Problem

People scroll for hours every day — mornings, commutes, breaks, before bed. This time is spent on social media feeds optimized for engagement, not growth. Meanwhile, the books, articles, and videos people consume get forgotten within weeks. There's no tool that turns your personal knowledge library into a feed that's as addictive as social media but actually makes you smarter.

## Solution

Scrollect is a personal learning feed. You add the content you consume — books, articles, tweets, YouTube videos, PDFs — and an AI agent transforms it into a scrollable stream of bite-sized cards. The feed includes:

- **Key insights and takeaways** extracted from your content
- **Spaced-repetition prompts** to help you retain what you've learned
- **Cross-source connections** ("This article echoes what Martin Fowler said in...")
- **Relevant quotes** surfaced at the right time

The AI learns from your interactions (save, like, dislike) to continuously improve what it shows you.

### Connected Knowledge

Inspired by tools like Obsidian, Scrollect treats your content as a connected graph — not isolated items. A tag system lets you (and the AI) organize and link content across sources. Tags create visible threads through your library, and the feed uses them to surface connections you wouldn't spot on your own ("3 of your sources tagged #system-design agree on this principle, but this article challenges it"). Over time, your tag map becomes a personal knowledge graph you can browse and explore.

## Target User

Software engineers who are active learners — they read technical books, follow industry blogs, watch conference talks, and bookmark tweets. They want to retain and connect what they learn, but don't have a system for it.

## Key Principles

- **Personal, not social.** No followers, no comments, no public profiles. This is your private learning space.
- **Scroll-native.** Don't fight the scroll — use it. The UX should feel as effortless as Instagram or Twitter.
- **AI-first.** The feed is generated by a dedicated AI agent that knows your library and learning patterns.
- **Low friction input.** Adding content should take seconds — browser extension, file upload, paste a URL.

## MVP Scope

### Web App

- User accounts and personal library
- Add content: PDF, EPUB, YouTube URL, article URL, raw text
- AI-generated scroll feed with mixed card types (insights, quizzes, connections, quotes)
- Card interactions: save, like, dislike
- Tagging system — AI auto-suggests tags on import, user can create/edit tags manually
- Tag-based browsing and filtering (explore your library by topic)
- AI-powered semantic search — natural language queries across your entire library (e.g. "what did I read about error handling in distributed systems?")
- Feed algorithm adapts based on interactions and tag relationships

### Browser Extension

- "Send to Scrollect" button for any webpage
- Capture tweets, articles, blog posts with one click

### Out of Scope (for MVP)

- Mobile native apps
- Social features
- Content creation / note-taking
- Integrations with Kindle, Readwise, etc.

## Technical Direction

- Web app (likely Next.js or similar)
- LLM APIs for content processing and feed generation (e.g. Claude API)
- 3rd-party APIs where useful to speed up MVP (e.g. article extraction, YouTube transcripts)
- Vector database for semantic search across the user's content library
- Browser extension (Chrome, initially)

## Open Questions

- How to handle large content (full books) — chunking strategy, summarization depth?
- What's the right card generation cadence — on-demand as you scroll, or pre-generated batches?
- How much of the book/article should the AI "read" vs. relying on existing summaries/metadata?
- Optimal spaced-repetition intervals for this format?

## Monetization

Free for the MVP phase. Monetization strategy to be defined after validating the core experience.
